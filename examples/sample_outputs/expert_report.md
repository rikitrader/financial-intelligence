## IV. Methodology

My analysis employed the following methodologies:

### A. Statistical Analysis
- **Benford's Law**: First and second digit analysis to detect manipulation

- **Pattern Recognition**: Clustering analysis for related transactions

### B. Forensic Procedures
- **Transaction Tracing**: Following funds from initiation to settlement
- **Entity Resolution**: Matching and linking related parties
- **Timeline Reconstruction**: Sequencing events chronologically

## V. Findings

### Finding 1: Currency Structuring Pattern

**Opinion:** Based on my analysis, it is my opinion that the cash deposit pattern observed constitutes potential structuring under 31 U.S.C. ยง 5324.

**Facts:**
- January 18, 2024: Cash deposit $9,999.00 (Ref: CASH001)
- January 19, 2024: Cash deposit $9,998.00 (Ref: CASH002)
- January 20, 2024: Cash deposit $9,997.00 (Ref: CASH003)
- February 12, 2024: Cash deposit $9,999.00 (Ref: CASH004)

**Analysis:**
The probability of four deposits on three consecutive days (and one additional) all falling within $3 of the $10,000 CTR threshold by chance is less than 0.01%. The pattern is consistent with intentional structuring to avoid currency transaction reporting.

**Basis for Opinion:**
- Statistical improbability of coincidence
- Consistent with FinCEN structuring typology
- No documented business purpose for cash operations
- Same individual (rjones) conducted all deposits

---

### Finding 2: Unauthorized Offshore Transfers

**Opinion:** Based on my analysis, three wire transfers to Offshore Holdings Ltd violated corporate policy and exhibit characteristics of suspicious activity.

**Facts:**
- January 20, 2024: Wire $9,500.00 to Cayman National Bank
- February 5, 2024: Wire $9,400.00 to Cayman National Bank
- February 20, 2024: Wire $9,300.00 to Cayman National Bank

**Analysis:**
Corporate policy (EX-103) requires board approval for all foreign transfers. No evidence of board authorization was provided. The descending amount pattern suggests awareness of potential monitoring thresholds.

**Basis for Opinion:**
- Documentary evidence of policy violation
- High-risk jurisdiction (Cayman Islands)
- No documented business purpose
- Descending amount pattern

---

### Finding 3: Ghost Employee Payments

**Opinion:** Based on my analysis, payments to employee E010 ("Ghost Employee") lack supporting documentation and exhibit characteristics of payroll fraud.

**Facts:**
- Four bi-weekly payments totaling $18,000
- Employee listed in Operations department
- No HR records, badge access, or email activity found

**Analysis:**
The absence of any corroborating evidence of employment, combined with the unusual employee name, strongly suggests fictitious payroll entries.

**Basis for Opinion:**
- Lack of employment verification
- No physical presence indicators
- Suspicious employee naming
- Pattern consistent with ghost employee schemes
